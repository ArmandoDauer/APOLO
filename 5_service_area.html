Target Passengers

Another element defined early in the design process is the primary group(s) the service is intended to serve effectively. The inefficiencies perceived by stakeholders may either affect the general public or a specific segment of the population. In the first case, the system should be designed to serve society, maximizing the number of passengers that can benefit from the service. In the second case, the system focuses on meeting the needs of a particular group, and certain features of the service must be tailored to accommodate the specific requirements of that group. Through our review, we identified three different types of passengers, listed below.

  • General Passengers: The whole population is considered as potential passengers of the system. There will be no design concession for favoring a specific group.
  • Population in Need (low-income, impaired, elderly): This population typically includes individuals with physical impairments who often lack access to other transportation options. The most common trip purposes for this group are medical appointments, shopping, and leisure activities. This demographic generally places a higher value on comfort (such as door-to-door and direct service) rather than strict punctuality.
  • Time Sensitive Passengers (youth in scholar age or working population):Typically composed of younger and economically active individuals, this population often uses transport for work and school-related activities. Since these activities are usually time-sensitive, this demographic tends to prioritize punctuality over comfort.
